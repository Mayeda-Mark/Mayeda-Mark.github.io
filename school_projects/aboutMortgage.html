About Mortgages

What is a mortgage?

A mortgage is a type of loan from a financial institution designed specifically for the purpose of purchasing a home. Essentially, the bank is the actual purchasing entity of the home and maintains ownership of the home until the loan has been paid in full.

Terms

Below is a list of terms which are generally associated with mortages:

  • Principle: The principle is the total dollar amont of the loan.
  • Interest rate: Also called APR, interest rate is the anual interest rate of the loan, or the amount that the loan grown in value over the course of a year.
  • Period: Also called a term, a period is the frequency that a payment is due on the loan, For example, a loan with due every month has 12 periods per year.
  • Down Payment: A down payment is the amount of money that the purchaser puts toward the purchase of the home.

Additional Information

Below is additional information regarding mortgage loans which can be helpful:

  • The generally accepted minimum down payment that a financial institution will accept is 3 percent of the principle.
  • Interest rates are set by the Federal Reserve and are based on which state you live in, but can change due to many factors including credit score.
